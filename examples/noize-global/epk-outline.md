# DJ Noize — EPK Outline (One-Page Blueprint)

A fill-in structure for the electronic press kit. Promoters skim an EPK in under 60 seconds looking for one thing: evidence you'll fill their room and won't be a problem to work with. Every section below earns its space or gets cut.

## 1. Hook / Positioning Line

One sentence at the very top, before the name is even fully processed. This is the line a talent buyer forwards to their partner — make it a credit-backed claim, not an adjective pile.

**Suggested line:**
> "DJ Noize is the Billboard #1 producer behind records for TLC, Snoop Dogg, and Jagged Edge — 20 years of studio ears, now reading the room from the booth."

## 2. Bio — Short (≤75 words) + Long (≤250 words)

Two versions: the short one gets pasted into event listings and flyers verbatim, so it must stand alone; the long one is for press and corporate decks. Lead with the Billboard #1 and the household-name credits (TLC, Snoop Dogg, Too Short, Ne-Yo/Christina Milian in *Step Up 2*), then bridge to the DJ story — producers who DJ signal deep crates and technical reliability, which is exactly what buyers want to hear.

## 3. Credits / Highlights Bar

A single scannable strip of 5–7 proof points (logos or bold text): Billboard #1 · TLC · Snoop Dogg · Raedio (Issa Rae) · Amazon · Madewell · *Step Up 2*. Promoters and brand bookers make trust decisions off recognizable names in two seconds — this bar does more selling than any paragraph.

## 4. Mixes & Live Assets

One featured 30–45 min mix (link, not attachment) plus one 30–60 second live crowd clip per gig type (club energy, corporate polish, private-party dance floor). Buyers book what they can hear and *see working on a crowd* — the crowd clip is the single highest-converting asset in the kit, so keep it current (under 6 months old).

## 5. Press, Photos & Brand Work

3–4 high-res photos (one clean headshot, one action-in-the-booth, one wide crowd shot) plus one line each on the Madewell/Issa Rae commercial work, the Amazon Black History Month project, and The Song Builders role. This section answers the corporate buyer's real question — "has this person delivered inside a brand environment before?" — with a yes and receipts.

## 6. Stage & Tech Summary

Three lines max: preferred setup (e.g. 2x CDJ-3000 linked + DJM-series mixer, booth monitor), acceptable substitutions, and what he arrives with (USBs / self-contained sound for private and corporate up to 150 guests). A clear tech summary tells the promoter's production manager this will be an easy advance — easy advances get re-booked.

## 7. Contact & Booking

Name, booking email, phone, city (Los Angeles), one social/streaming link cluster, and a line pointing to the rate sheet ("Rates and packages available on request — quotes in writing within 24 hours"). One person, one email, no maze: buyers move to the next EPK the moment contact gets complicated.
